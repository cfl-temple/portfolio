Richard Lin is an undergraduate student in Electrical and Computer Engineering at Temple University and a Temple STEPS Scholar, recognized through this NSF-supported program for academic excellence and commitment to sustainability and energy innovation. He is passionate about robotics, artificial intelligence, sustainability, and software–hardware integration. Richard works as an undergraduate research assistant in the Computer Fusion Lab, developing movement systems for an assistive humanoid robot for health applications. He also manages the lab’s website, serves as system administrator, and is an E-board member of Temple’s IEEE student organization, supporting technical initiatives and student leadership.
